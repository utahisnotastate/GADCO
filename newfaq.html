How does the whole process work?

Step 1
Quote Creation:
We use your details to provide an accurate cost estimate for the job.

Step 2
Finalize minor details:
We work together to finalize all the customizable aspects, the stain color, hinge color, door swing direction, etc. You sign the quote form confirming the order.

Step 3
Pay deposit:
We collect a 50% deposit to confirm production.

Step 4
Technical drawing
creation:
We provide a technical drawing of the door and windows, and make any sketches for custom hand carving. This insures all the details and artistic direction match your vision.

Step 5
We build your door:
This phase generally takes six to ten weeks depending on the complexity of your door. We will send you regular updates and pictures throughout the production process.

Step 6
Crafting
Your finished door is placed inside a plywood crate for protection during shipment.

Step 7
Settle final balance:
We send you pictures of your completed door showing all details match your specifications. Once your final payment is received, the door is shipped.

Step 8
Receipt and inspection:
You receive and inspect the door to insure no damages have incurred during shipping.

What is the difference between pre-hung doors and door slabs?

Door slabs are just doors without the frame. Pre-hung doors refer to door slabs that come with custom fit doorframes, thresholds, and interior and exterior molding.

How do we size a door?

We are able to build any size door to match your projectâ€™s specific requirements. If you are ordering a flat top door slab we need vertical and horizontal measurements. For doors with round tops we additionally need a vertical spring point measurement. Door slabs are not predrilled or mortised for hardware.


Pre-hung doors can be made to a specific size and rough openings will be supplied or you can give us rough opening specifications and we will build a door to fit inside. Pre-hung doors come with hinges and a door slab prepped for hinges.

How is the wood treated?

All of our doors are treated with a state of the art carbonization process tailored specifically for your door that protects your product from warping, temperature fluctuations, humid climates, and microbes. Despite all precautions, long-term exposure to direct sunlight and/or precipitation can damage the doors so we strongly recommend installing exterior doors in areas with an overhang at least half the distance the door is tall.

Do the doors come with hardware?

All of our pre-hung doors come with hinges. Besides hinges we can help you source and select any kind of hardware you need for your project. The hardware will be used to prep your door to your specifications, so they can be easily installed on site when your door arrives.
