FAQ

  • Who we help
  • At Grand Approach we take pride in helping a large range of consumers. Homeowners, homebuilders, remodelers, contractors, architects, even church and school care takers can all benefit from working with us.
  • How long will it take to receive my door?
  • The entire process takes 8 – 12 weeks depending on complexity and individual needs. Once an order is placed we take the utmost care to provide you with an exquisite product, this care often translates to longer lead times but your patience will pay off.
  • What wood species do you use?
  • Currently we provide doors made from: Mahogany, Alder, and Pacific Cherry.
  • Do you install the door?
  • Our services do not include installation. Installation will need to be performed by a professional in your local area.
  • What is the difference between pre-hung doors and door slabs?
  • Door slabs are just doors without the frame. Pre-hung doors refer to door slabs that come with custom fit doorframes, thresholds, and interior and exterior molding.

  • How does the process work?:
  • Quote creation: We use your details to provide an accurate cost estimate for the job.
  • Finalize minor details: We work together to finalize all the customizable aspects, the stain color, hinge color, door swing direction, etc. You sign the quote form confirming the order.
  • Pay deposit:We collect a 50% deposit to confirm production.
  • Technical drawing creation: We provide a technical drawing of the door and windows, and make any sketches for custom hand carving. This insures all the details and artistic direction match your vision.
  • We build your door: This phase generally takes six to ten weeks depending on the complexity of your door. We will send you regular updates and pictures throughout the production process.
  • Crating: Your finished door is placed inside a plywood crate for protection during shipment.
  • Settle final balance: We send you pictures of your completed door showing all details match your specifications. Once your final payment is received, the door is shipped.
  • Receipt and inspection: You receive and inspect the door to insure no damages have incurred during shipping.
